The Company is the Intelligence

Dorsey's thesis: hierarchy exists to route information. AI routes information better. So the hierarchy should go — and the company should be rebuilt as a living world model with humans at the edges.

Source: "From Hierarchy to Intelligence" — Block / Sequoia Podcast, Mar–Apr 2026

The Core Thesis

Hierarchy is an information routing protocol. AI does it better.

If you look at it from first principles, hierarchy is all about information flow to a broad base of people... And now we're facing a completely foundational moment in being able to question every element of how we work.

Jack Dorsey, Sequoia Podcast, April 2026

The Roman army's contubernium (8 soldiers → decanus) through to today's corporate org chart: all are the same solution to one problem — how do you route information across thousands of people? Middle management exists to relay context up and down. In a remote-first company where every action creates a machine-readable artifact (Slack, PRs, docs, emails), an AI can do this continuously and losslessly.

System Architecture

Four layers. Intelligence at the core.

Instead of products with roadmaps, the company is built as four composable layers. The intelligence layer orchestrates everything — it doesn't need product managers hypothesizing; customer reality generates the backlog.

1

Foundation

Capabilities

Atomic primitives the company does well. No UI of their own. Hard to acquire, have network effects or regulatory moats. These are the building blocks everything else composes from.

PaymentsLendingCard IssuanceBankingBNPLPayroll → For your org: APIs, core functions, data processing, specialized tools
2

Brain

World Model (Dual)

Two sides: the Company World Model (what's being built, what's blocked, resource allocation — replaces what managers used to know) + the Customer World Model (per-customer understanding from honest signal that compounds daily).

All artifacts ingested: Slack, PRs, docs, emails, meetings Continuously updated Queryable by anyone at any layer Predictive over time
3

Orchestrator

Intelligence Layer

Composes capabilities into solutions for specific customers at specific moments — proactively, without a product manager deciding. When it can't compose something because a capability doesn't exist, that gap becomes the roadmap.

No predetermined roadmap Composes capabilities in real-time Proactive — surfaces insights before asked Gap → new capability signal

Delivery Surface

Interfaces

Hardware and software that touches the real world. Important, but not where value is created. The interface is just the delivery surface for composed solutions. Can be dynamic/composed in real-time in the future.

Org Structure

Three roles. No permanent middle management.

Block is normalizing down to three roles. The world model provides the context that managers used to provide — so ICs can act without waiting for information to travel up and down a chain.

⚒️

Individual Contributor

IC / Builder

Engineers, designers, salespeople, operators. Build and operate capabilities, the model, the intelligence layer, and interfaces. Each person is AI-augmented — can do what a team of 10 used to do.

Durable Human Skill

Judgment · Taste · Creativity

🎯

Directly Responsible Individual

DRI

Owns a specific customer outcome or cross-cutting problem, with a time-bound mandate (e.g. 90 days on merchant churn). Full authority to pull resources across teams. Strategy lives here, not in management layers.

Durable Human Skill

Ownership · Accountability

🤝

Player-Coach

Player-Coach

Still writes code, builds, designs — but also develops people around them by doing the work, not just directing it. Replaces info-routing managers. Assignment-based, not a reporting structure.

Durable Human Skill

Human Capacity · Coaching · Empathy

Before vs. After

Old Hierarchy

Pyramid structure — CEO top, ICs at bottom. 5+ layers deep.

Information filtered up and down by managers, losing fidelity at each hop.

Roadmap by product managers hypothesizing what customers want.

Managers coordinate — status meetings, alignment sessions, priority negotiations.

Politics and agenda determine what gets built. Loudest table-pounder wins.

Company legibility = 30–40%. The CEO never truly knows what's happening.

Intelligence-Centered

Circle structure — World model at center, humans at the edge. 2–3 layers max.

World model routes information continuously, losslessly, queryable by anyone.

Roadmap by customer reality — gaps in the intelligence layer surface what to build next.

AI coordinates — the world model gives everyone the context they need to act without waiting.

Signal determines priority — transaction data and queries show truth, not opinion.

Company fully legible — CEO and everyone else can query the company's state in real time.

How to Build It

Personal use & organizational playbook

Capture all artifacts into one system

Every note, conversation, decision, project, and document should flow into a single knowledge base. This is your personal world model's raw material. The key is machine-readability — plain text or markdown, not PDFs and slides buried in folders.

ObsidianNotionLogseqApple Notes + iCloudRoam Research

Build your personal world model with a graph

Link people, projects, decisions, and outcomes with typed relationships. Not flat files — a graph. A decision links to the project it belongs to, the people involved, the date, the outcome. When you query it later, you get full context.

Obsidian (graph view)Logseq (knowledge graph)Neo4j (technical)Capacities

Layer AI on top for querying and synthesis

Point an LLM at your knowledge base so you can have a conversation with your own data. "What decisions did I make about Project X and why?" "What's been blocking me this quarter?" This is you querying your own world model.

NotebookLM (Google)Cursor / Composer on your vaultClaude with MCP file accessGPT + file uploads

Add proactive intelligence via agents

Build lightweight agents that watch your data and surface insights before you ask. A weekly "state of you" digest. A prompt when a project has been stalled for 7 days. Automated summaries of decisions made this month. This mirrors Block's proactive intelligence layer.

n8n (self-hosted)Make.comZapier + OpenAIClaude MCP agentsGoose (Block's own tool)

Establish a single source of truth

All decisions, discussions, code, designs, plans, and progress must flow into one place. The company is only legible to AI if the raw material is machine-readable and centralized. Remote-first culture helps — everything creates an artifact by default.

Linear (engineering)Notion / Confluence (docs)Slack (archived + indexed)GitHub (code artifacts)Loom (async meetings)

Build the Company Graph

Map every person, project, capability, customer segment, and outcome as nodes. Relationships between them (person → owns → project, project → relates to → customer problem) form the graph. This is the structural backbone of your world model. Start manually, then automate ingestion.

NOAN (purpose-built)Notion graph DBNeo4j + LangChainCustom knowledge graph

Deploy role-specific AI agents

Give each team member an AI agent configured to their role with appropriate data access. A CEO agent has full read access. An IC agent has access to their domain's artifacts. Each queries the same world model but through a lens. Per Eric Siu's playbook: fewer, more capable agents over many narrow ones.

Claude + MCPOpenAI Assistants APICursor (engineers)Custom GPT per role

Replace status meetings with queryable intelligence

Board meetings, 1:1s, and syncs exist to route information. Once the world model is queryable, replace standup formats with "query the company." Anyone can ask "what's blocked in engineering this week?" or "what customer problems have we not addressed?" The meeting becomes creative, not informational.

Weekly AI digest (automated)Slack-based query botDashboard with live model state

Audit information flow — find the lossy hops

Map where information currently lives and how many hops it takes to reach decision-makers. Every hop is a loss. Every meeting that exists to relay context is a candidate for elimination. Prioritize making the company's operations machine-readable first before layering AI.

Org topology auditArtifact inventory (what's recorded vs. lost)Decision latency mapping

Define your "honest signal" — what's your money?

Dorsey's advantage is transaction data — money doesn't lie. For your org: what is the equivalent high-frequency, high-fidelity signal that reveals customer truth? Product usage events, support tickets, NPS responses, sales call recordings — but what compounds and can't be gamed?

Product telemetry (usage events)Support ticket clusteringSales call transcriptsRevenue motion data

Build the Customer World Model separately

This is distinct from the company model. It's per-customer or per-segment understanding that deepens every time they interact. Build a data pipeline that continuously enriches customer profiles from honest signal. The goal is predictive — knowing what a customer needs before they ask.

Customer data platform (CDP)dbt + data warehouseLLM-enriched customer profilesSegment / RudderStack

Pilot DRI structure before full restructure

Don't eliminate hierarchy overnight. Run a DRI pilot: assign one cross-functional owner to a specific customer problem for 90 days, with explicit authority to pull resources. Measure decision latency vs. traditional PM process. Use this as proof-of-concept before org-wide restructure. Dorsey himself cut 40% only after 12 months of experimentation.

One DRI trial per quarterExplicit resource authorityTime-boxed mandate (90 days)
